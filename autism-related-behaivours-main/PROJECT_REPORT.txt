2. INTRODUCTION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) is a neurodevelopmental condition characterized by 
persistent challenges in social interaction, communication, and the presence of 
repetitive, restricted patterns of behavior. Early identification of these 
traits—particularly Motor Stereotyped Behaviors (MSBs)—is critical, as the 
first few years of a child's life represent a "golden window" for intervention.

"EARLY SCREEN ASD" emerges at the intersection of pediatric healthcare and 
artificial intelligence. The project aims to modernize the screening process by 
replacing subjective, manual observation with objective, high-precision computer 
vision analysis. By leveraging Temporal Convolutional Networks, our system 
can identify and quantify behaviors like Arm Flapping, Head Banging, and 
Spinning with a level of consistency and speed that is difficult to achieve 
through human observation alone.


3. ABSTRACT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Early identification of Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) is crucial for improving 
long-term outcomes through early intervention. However, clinical diagnosis is 
often delayed due to the subjective nature of observation and high resource 
demands. "EARLY SCREEN ASD" presents an automated, computer-vision-based 
solution for screening Motor Stereotyped Behaviors (MSBs). 

The system employs a dual-stage neural architecture: 
1. **Spatial Feature Extraction**: A pre-trained ResNet18 model is used to 
   extract 512-dimensional spatial feature vectors from video frames.
2. **Temporal Modeling**: A Temporal Convolutional Network (TCN) with dilated 
   convolutions and a large receptive field analyzes sequences of these features 
   to identify patterns such as Arm Flapping, Head Banging, and Body Spinning.

The resulting tool is a professional desktop application that provides 
conclusive clinical reports, high-resolution frame previews, and objective 
confidence margins, bridging the gap between advanced AI research and 
practical diagnostic assistance.


4. ABOUT THE PROJECT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3.1 PROBLEM STATEMENT:
The clinical diagnosis of Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) is historically marred by 
significant delays, often referred to as the "Diagnostic Lag." While early signs of 
atypical development can be observed as early as 18 months, children frequently 
do not receive a formal diagnosis until age 4 or 5. 

The primary bottlenecks in the current screening system include:
- **Subjective observation**: Dependence on the recall of parents or the 
  manual timing of behaviors by specialists, which is prone to error.
- **Resource scarcity**: A global shortage of developmental specialists leading 
  to waitlists that can span years.
- **High Resource intensity**: Manual behavioral analysis requires hours of 
  observation by highly trained (and expensive) professionals.

3.2 PROJECT OBJECTIVES:
"EARLY SCREEN ASD" was developed to bridge this gap by providing an automated, 
objective, and high-speed preliminary screening tool. The core objectives are:
- **Reduction of Diagnostic Lag**: Enable parents and pediatricians to perform 
  rapid, home-based or primary-care-based behavioral screenings.
- **Objective Quantification**: Replace stopwatches and clickers with AI that 
  detects rhythmic Motor Stereotyped Behaviors (MSBs) with sub-second accuracy.
- **Clinical Documentation**: Generate professional, clinical-grade reports that 
  can be shared with specialists to speed up the formal diagnostic process.
- **False Positive Mitigation**: Implement safety features to ensure the tool 
  remains focused on human behavior, preventing errors from non-human sources.

3.3 PROJECT SCOPE:
The scope of this project is concentrated on the detection and analysis of 
Motor Stereotyped Behaviors (MSBs), which are among the earliest and most recognizable 
markers of ASD.
- **Behaviors Covered**: Arm Flapping, Head Banging, and Body Spinning.
- **Technology Focus**: Uses Temporal Convolutional Networks (TCN) to understand 
  the *rhythm* of movement, not just the single image.
- **Clinical Positioning**: This is explicitly a **SCREENING TOOL**. It provides 
  statistical evidence of traits but does not issue a final medical diagnosis.
- **Local Analysis**: All video processing is done locally on the user's machine 
  to ensure the highest level of patient privacy and data security.

3.4 FEASIBILITY STUDY:
- **Technical Feasibility**: The project utilizes a pre-trained ResNet18 backbone 
  combined with a custom TCN. By implementing Batch Processing, the system 
  reaches real-time performance on standard consumer-grade CPUs and GPUs.
- **Operational Feasibility**: The interface is designed for clinical "Zero-Distraction." 
  A user can upload a video and receive a report in three clicks, requiring No 
  specialized technical knowledge from the parent or educator.
- **Economic Feasibility**: By automating the observation phase, it significantly 
  reduces the cost barrier for initial screening, potentially saving families 
  thousands in early-stage consultation fees.

4.5 DATA SECURITY & PATIENT PRIVACY:
Given the sensitive nature of pediatric behavioral data, "EARLY SCREEN ASD" is 
built with a "Privacy-First" architecture. 
- **Localized Computing (Edge AI)**: All video processing, feature extraction, 
  and behavioral analysis are performed entirely on the user's local machine. 
  No video data is ever transmitted to external servers or the cloud.
- **Zero Cloud Footprint**: The application does not require an active internet 
  connection for screening, preventing data breaches from network vulnerabilities.
- **Controlled Data Lifecycle**: Results and PDF reports are stored in user-selected 
  directories, giving the parent or clinician full control over data disposal.
- **Compliance Alignment**: This decentralized approach aligns with the core 
  principles of HIPAA (Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act) and 
  GDPR by minimizing data exposure and ensuring patient confidentiality.

6.4 BEHAVIORAL SCORING LOGIC (CUMULATIVE ASD):
Total_ASD_Pct = Sum(Armflapping, Headbanging, Spinning)
1. If Total_ASD_Pct >= 70% -> "AUTISM POSITIVE (Conclusive)"
2. If Dominant_ASD >= 50% and RunnerUp_ASD >= 40% -> "AUTISM POSITIVE (Strong Mixed ASD Traits)"
3. If Total_ASD_Pct >= 50% -> "MODERATE AUTISM CHANGES"
4. If Total_ASD_Pct < 40% and Normal_Pct >= 60% -> "NORMAL (No ASD Traits Detected)"
5. Else -> "INCONCLUSIVE (Low MSB Confidence)"


7. PROGRAM DESIGN & COMPONENT SPECIFICATIONS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The "Early Screen ASD" system is designed as a modular, high-performance desktop 
application. The architecture prioritizes "Edge-AI" (local processing) to 
ensure patient privacy while maintaining a professional, zero-distraction 
clinical interface.

7.1 ARCHITECTURAL PRINCIPLES:
The system follows a Decoupled Controller Architecture, where the User 
Interface (Frontend), Behavioral Analysis (Inference), and Clinical 
Documentation (Reporting) operate as independent but strictly interfaced units. 
This ensures that updates to the AI model do not require a rewrite of the UI 
logic, and vice versa.

7.2 CORE COMPONENT DEEP-DIVE:
1. **Frontend Module (AutismBehaviorApp)**:
   - **Page Manager**: Implements a custom state-machine to transition between 
     the "Home" and "Screening" pages without memory-intensive reloading.
   - **Event-Driven UX**: Utilizing Tkinter's main loop, the system remains 
     responsive during heavy frame decoding by offloading tasks to worker threads.
   - **Real-Time Video Rendering**: Uses OpenCV and PIL (Pillow) to decode 
     H.264 streams and map them to high-resolution Tkinter canvas elements 
     with frame-accurate seek capabilities.

2. **Inference Engine (ResNet-TCN Pipeline)**:
   - **Spatial Backbone**: A ResNet-18 model (pre-trained on ImageNet) acts as 
     the "Eye" of the system, converting raw pixel data into 512-dimensional 
     semantic vectors.
   - **Temporal Brain**: A custom Temporal Convolutional Network (TCN) analyzes 
     the *vibration* of these vectors over 10-frame windows (approx. 1.6s). 
     The model classifies clips into 4 categories: Arm Flapping, Head Banging, 
     Spinning, or Normal Activity.
- **Cumulative Batch Optimization**: To achieve real-time speed, the engine 
     stacks frames into Tensor Batches (Size=32), utilizing SIMD/GPU 
     acceleration to process an entire video clip in seconds.

3. **Reporting Engine (ASDReportPDF)**:
   - **Data-to-Clinical Mapping**: Translates raw floating-point probabilities 
     into professional PDF charts and qualitative clinical verdicts.
   - **Automated Verification**: Generates a unique Report ID and timestamp 
     to ensure data traceability in clinical archives.

7.3 CONCURRENCY & THREADING MODEL:
To prevent the application from "freezing" during analysis, the system 
implements a Multi-Threaded Execution Model:
- **UI Thread (Main)**: Handles user clicks, window resizing, and the 
  smooth 25-FPS video preview.
- **Analysis Thread (Worker)**: Executes the PyTorch inference pipeline 
  and communicates progress back to the UI via a thread-safe Queue/Callback 
  mechanism. This ensures the PROGRESS BAR remains fluid even during 
  100% CPU/GPU utilization.

7.4 SYSTEM SAFETY & ERROR MITIGATION:
- **False Positive Prevention**: Before behavioral analysis, a validation 
  pass checks for "Human-like Motion" to prevent the AI from misinterpreting 
  background objects or pets as clinical traits.
- **Padding & Boundary Control**: For videos with non-standard lengths, 
  the system applies "Reflection Padding" to the feature vectors, ensuring 
  the TCN never encounters null-input errors.

7.5 ALGORITHM SPECIFICATION (SMART-VOTING):
```python
# The logical heart of the decision-making process:
Function Analyze_Behavior(Video_V):
    1. Frames = Decode(V, skip=5) 
    2. Spatial_Features = ResNet18(Frames)
    3. Clips = Window(Spatial_Features, size=10, stride=5) # 50% Overlap
    4. Probs = TCN_Inference(Clips) # 4xN Output
    
    # Cumulative Consensus Logic
    Total_ASD_Pct = Sum(P[Arm], P[Head], P[Spin])
    Normal_Pct = P[Normal]
    
    # Verdict Mapping
    If Total_ASD_Pct >= 70%: Return "AUTISM POSITIVE (Conclusive)"
    If Dominant >= 50% & RunnerUp >= 40%: Return "AUTISM POSITIVE (Mixed)"
    If Total_ASD_Pct >= 50%: Return "MODERATE AUTISM CHANGES"
    If Normal_Pct >= 60%: Return "NORMAL ACTIVITY"
    Else: Return "INCONCLUSIVE"
```

11. WORKFLOW OF THE EARLY SCREEN ASD COMPUTER VISION–BASED BEHAVIORAL SCREENING SYSTEM
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The system operates as a cohesive end-to-end pipeline, transforming raw video data 
into structured clinical intelligence. The workflow is divided into five 
mathematically and logically distinct phases:

PHASE 1: VIDEO PREPROCESSING & NORMALIZATION
- **Frame Selection**: The system decodes the video stream and implements a 
  `FRAME_SKIP=5` logic to reduce computational redundancy while preserving the 
  essential temporal rhythm of the behavior.
- **Normalization**: Each selected frame is resized to 224x224 pixels and 
  normalized using ImageNet means (μ=[0.485, 0.456, 0.406]) to ensure 
  compatibility with the pre-trained spatial backbone.
- **Subject Validation**: A "Human Presence" check scans for skeletal structure 
  or human facial features to prevent the analysis of static backgrounds.

PHASE 2: DEEP SPATIAL FEATURE EXTRACTION (RESNET-18)
- **Architecture**: Frames are passed through a 18-layer Deep Residual Network.
- **Feature Vector**: The model removes the final classification layer and 
  extracts the 512-dimensional output from the Global Average Pooling layer.
- **Result**: Each frame is now represented as a compact point in a 512-D 
  latent space, capturing the child's posture and movement position.

PHASE 3: SEQUENTIAL TEMPORAL MODELING (TCN)
- **Windowing**: Features are grouped into sequences of 10 (CLIP_LENGTH=10).
- **Dilated Convolutions**: The TCN uses causal convolutions with exponentially 
  increasing dilation factors (1, 2, 4, 8...). This allows the model to analyze 
  patterns over longer durations without losing fine-grained detail.
- **Pattern Recognition**: The model identifies rhythmic frequencies specific 
  to ASD traits (e.g., the high-frequency oscillation of arm flapping).

- **Sliding Window**: The system slides across the video with a 5-unit stride, 
  ensuring 50% temporal overlap.
- **Voting Mechanism**: Every 10-frame clip issues probabilities for the 
  4 behavior classes (Armflapping, Headbanging, Spinning, and Normal).
- **Cumulative Aggregation**: A conclusive verdict is reached by summing ALL 
  ASD trait percentages. This allows the system to detect complex, mixed 
  stimmings that would be missed by simple majority voting.
- **Moderate Risk Layer**: An intermediate alert level ('MODERATE') captures 
  emerging traits that do not yet meet the 70% diagnostic threshold but 
  warrant clinical follow-up.

PHASE 5: AUTOMATED REPORT GENERATION & DATA EXPORT
- **Report Synthesis**: The `ASDReportPDF` engine aggregates the dominance 
  scores and generates a professional medical report. 
- **Validation**: The final output includes time-stamped report IDs and 
  technical specifications for hardware verification.
- **User Delivery**: The report is saved locally, and the user is provided 
  with the option to launch the PDF immediately for review.
